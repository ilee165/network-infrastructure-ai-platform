# Review: ADR-0030 (Backup & DR Baseline) and ADR-0032 (KMS-Backed Master Key)

**Reviewer pass:** internal consistency, gaps, and conflicts with the `DECISIONS-BRIEF` D-decisions
**Date:** 2026-06-21
**Status of reviewed ADRs:** both **Proposed**, P1 W0, dated 2026-06-20
**Inputs:** `docs/adr/0030-backup-and-disaster-recovery-baseline.md`, `docs/adr/0032-kms-backed-master-key-and-rotation.md`, `docs/architecture/DECISIONS-BRIEF.md` (D1–D16), with corroboration from ADR-0011, ADR-0023, and `docs/roadmap/PRODUCTION.md` (§5, §7, §8, §11).

## Verdict

Both ADRs are individually well-argued and each is, on its own, consistent with the relevant D-decisions (D4, D5, D11, D14). The problems are almost entirely **at the seam between them**: ADR-0030's credential-recovery story depends on ADR-0032's KEK lifecycle, but the two were written as if the other did not exist — **neither ADR references the other** (ADR-0030 says the KEK is "KMS/file-provided (ADR-0011 §1)" and never cites ADR-0032; ADR-0032 never mentions backup, restore, or DR). Two of the findings below (F1, F2) are latent **DR-correctness defects** that a green restore drill would not catch. The rest are gaps and wording fixes.

No finding contradicts a binding D-decision; the D-alignment is sound. The risk is in the operational coupling the brief does not adjudicate (DR ↔ key lifecycle), which is exactly what these two ADRs are supposed to pin down jointly.

## F1 — Critical: backup retention can outlive KEK-version retention → undecryptable restore

**Where:** ADR-0030 §1 (retention "35 days of fulls+incrementals + monthly fulls kept 1 year") and §5 drill 1(c); ADR-0032 §3 (re-wrap retires old KEK versions once "the job confirms zero rows reference it"; Vault Transit raises `min_decryption_version`).

**Problem.** A pgBackRest backup freezes `device_credentials(wrapped_dek, kek_version)` at a point in time. To decrypt any of those rows on restore, the KMS must still hold **every `kek_version` referenced inside that backup**. ADR-0032 §3 retires an old KEK version when **zero live rows** reference it — but a backup taken *before* a rotation still references the old version, and that backup lives for up to **1 year** (monthly fulls). The "zero rows reference it" check looks only at the live table, never at frozen rows inside retained backups. Consequence:

> A master-key rotation completes, the old KEK version is retired (AWS old backing-key deleted / Vault `min_decryption_version` raised), and **every Postgres backup taken before that rotation becomes undecryptable for credentials** — even though `pgbackrest verify` is clean and the restore itself succeeds. The platform comes back inert for device access *and cannot be made operational* for any credential created before the last surviving KEK version, with no error until someone tries to unwrap.

ADR-0030 §5 drill 1(c) ("a credential row decrypts only with the matching KEK and fails closed without it") would **pass** against a fresh backup whose `kek_version` is still active, so the drill does not surface this. ADR-0032 §3 even reasons about cutover safety using only the live worklist predicate.

**Fix (jointly owned).** Add an invariant binding the two retention policies: **KEK-version retention ≥ longest backup retention** (here ≥ 1 year + WAL horizon). Concretely:
- ADR-0032 §3: do not retire a KEK version while any **retained backup** may reference it — gate cutover on backup retention, not just live-row count. For Vault Transit, keep `min_decryption_version` below the oldest version present in retained backups; for AWS, never delete a CMK/backing key still referenced by a retained backup.
- ADR-0030 §1/§5: add a drill assertion that restores the **oldest retained full** and decrypts a sample credential, so version-retention drift fails the drill, not production DR.

## F2 — Major: no end-to-end "restore + KEK-escrow recovery" drill; ownership falls between the two ADRs

**Where:** ADR-0030 §1 ("[KEK] recovery is the separate 'Secrets/master key' key-escrow drill … out of pgBackRest's scope"), §5 drill 3 ("restore Postgres from object storage **alone** onto a clean cluster"); ADR-0032 Negative consequences ("documented key-escrow/recovery procedure … key-recovery drill annually") and PRODUCTION.md §8 ("Secrets/master key — KMS-managed; key escrow procedure documented; Key-recovery drill annually").

**Problem.** A real total-loss DR scenario requires three things to be operational again: (1) restored Postgres, (2) a reachable/recovered KEK, (3) a proof that a credential actually decrypts. ADR-0030 designs (1) and explicitly pushes (2) out of scope; ADR-0032 mentions (2) only in its Negative consequences with **no procedure**; neither ADR designs (3) as a *combined* path. The §5.3 "full-platform DR drill" restores "from object storage **alone**" — by construction it proves recovery of a database that is **inert for device access** (ADR-0030 itself calls this the "intended" property), and it stops there.

The result is a coverage hole: the platform's headline DR claim ("RTO ≤ 1 h full platform, restore from backups alone") is demonstrated against a cluster that **cannot open a single device session** until a separate, undesigned, differently-scheduled (annual vs. quarterly/biannual) key-escrow drill happens to also work. "Restore works" and "platform is operational after DR" are not the same gate, and today nothing tests the second.

**Fix.** One of the two ADRs must own the combined path. Recommend ADR-0030 §5.3 take a dependency on the escrow recovery: the full-platform DR drill restores Postgres **and** recovers/reaches the KEK from escrow, then asserts a sampled credential decrypts end-to-end (closing F1's gap in the same step). ADR-0032 should specify the escrow/recovery procedure it currently only names, and cross-reference ADR-0030 §5 as the place it is exercised.

## F3 — Major: PROPOSED RPO ≤ 5 min is not deliverable from "continuous WAL archiving" alone

**Where:** ADR-0030 §1 (RPO row: "≤ 5 min (PROPOSED) — continuous WAL archiving"; "RPO from 'last full' down to 'last archived WAL segment'"), §6, Consequences ("P1's RPO floor is the WAL-archive interval, not replication lag"); PRODUCTION.md §8 targets line ("RPO ≤ 5 min (continuous WAL archiving **+ streaming replication**)").

**Problem.** A WAL segment is archived when it fills (default 16 MB) **or** when `archive_timeout` elapses. ADR-0030 specifies `archive_command`/`archive_mode = on` but **never sets `archive_timeout`**. During low write volume — common on a config/inventory platform at night — a segment can remain unfilled and unarchived well beyond 5 minutes, so the actual RPO is unbounded by writes, not by 5 minutes. The ADR is honest that streaming replication (the other half of PRODUCTION.md's stated mechanism) is P2, but it still claims the ≤ 5 min floor is "the WAL-archive interval," which is undefined without `archive_timeout`.

**Fix.** Make the RPO floor explicit and enforceable: set `archive_timeout ≤ RPO` (e.g. `archive_timeout = 60s`–`5min`) so an idle cluster still ships a (possibly partial) segment within the window, and state in §6 that P1's demonstrable RPO equals `archive_timeout`, with streaming replication tightening it in P2. The §5.1 PITR drill should assert the *worst-case* (idle-period) RPO, not just a busy-period replay.

## F4 — Minor: "KMS/file-provided" KEK wording conflicts with ADR-0032's production ban

**Where:** ADR-0030 §1 ("KMS/file-provided (ADR-0011 §1 `KeyProvider`)"), Alternatives #4 ("The KEK stays KMS/file-provided with its own escrow drill"); ADR-0032 §2 (`EnvKeyProvider`/`FileKeyProvider` cause the credential service to **refuse to start** under `is_prod`).

**Problem.** ADR-0030 describes the production DR path but repeatedly characterizes the KEK as "KMS/file-provided," implying a file KEK is an acceptable production source. ADR-0032 §2 makes that a hard fail-to-start in production. In the DR/restore context ADR-0030 addresses (a production cluster), the KEK is **KMS-only**; the file provider is dev/CI fallback. The wording is a residue of citing only ADR-0011 (which predates the prod ban) and not ADR-0032.

**Fix.** Replace "KMS/file-provided" with "KMS-provided (ADR-0032 §2; env/file providers are non-production fallback only)" wherever ADR-0030 discusses the production KEK, and cite ADR-0032.

## F5 — Minor: pcap-snapshot retention guarantee depends on the Celery coupling ADR-0030 §4 disclaims

**Where:** ADR-0030 §3 (snapshot "prunes from the object store any snapshot whose `pcap_metadata.tombstoned_at` is set") and §4 ("Celery beat is **not** used — these are infra jobs … keeps DR independent of app/broker health"); ADR-0023 §4 (the retention **Celery beat task** is what sets `tombstoned_at`).

**Problem.** `tombstoned_at` is written **only** by the Celery `packet`-queue beat job (ADR-0023 §4). If Celery/Redis/the packet worker is degraded — precisely the failure mode §4 says DR must be independent of — expired pcaps are never tombstoned, so the off-Celery snapshot CronJob never prunes them, and credential/PII-bearing payloads silently outlive their 30-day retention in the object store. The "shorter of (object-store policy) and (`retention_expires_at`)" rule is a partial backstop **only if** an object-store lifecycle policy is actually configured against `retention_expires_at`; the active prune path still hinges on a field the DR job cannot itself produce.

**Fix.** Make the snapshot job's prune self-sufficient: prune on `retention_expires_at < now()` (a field set at capture-complete, not by the beat job) in addition to `tombstoned_at`, and/or require the object-store lifecycle rule as a mandatory Helm value. State explicitly that DR retention-honoring must not depend on Celery liveness, consistent with §4's own rationale.

## F6 — Minor: audit-retention parenthetical is imprecise vs. the 7-year requirement

**Where:** ADR-0030 §1 retention row ("does **not** override the 7-year `audit_log` *data* retention (PRODUCTION.md §7) … audit rows live inside every Postgres backup, so audit data is retained as long as the longest-retained full"); PRODUCTION.md §7 (PROPOSED 7-year audit retention) and §8 (backup retention 35 days + monthly fulls 1 year).

**Problem.** The phrase "audit data is retained as long as the longest-retained full" (= 1 year) reads as if backup retention *satisfies* the 7-year audit requirement, which it does not. The requirement is actually met by the **live** system of record (audit_log "never purged within the retention window," PRODUCTION.md §7), with backups being point-in-time snapshots that each contain whatever audit history existed at capture time. The current wording invites the wrong conclusion (that 7-year-old audit history is recoverable from a backup, when only ≤ 1-year-old backups exist).

**Fix.** Reword to separate the two: live audit retention (7 years, system of record) vs. backup horizon (1 year). Optionally note that if DR must reconstruct >1-year-old audit history, that is a **live-DB** obligation, not a backup one — and flag whether the 1-year backup horizon is acceptable for an audit corpus that must survive 7 years (a Consultant/compliance question, not silently answered here).

## F7 — Minor: G-SEC "no plaintext credential in any backup sample" is ill-scoped against pcap snapshots

**Where:** ADR-0032 §4 / §6 and ADR-0030 cite G-SEC "no plaintext device credential in any API response, log, trace, or **backup sample**"; ADR-0030 §3 + ADR-0023 establish that pcap payloads contain **cleartext** credentials/PII **by design**, and the pcap volume is DR-snapshotted (a backup).

**Problem.** Read literally, a pcap DR snapshot is a "backup sample" that contains plaintext secrets, so the gate is either violated by design or undefined for this artifact class. The intent is clearly that the gate targets the **vault** (`device_credentials`, which must never appear in plaintext in the pgBackRest repo), not packet captures (which are access-gated diagnostic payloads under ADR-0023's separate contract).

**Fix.** Scope the G-SEC "backup sample" leak check to the **pgBackRest/Postgres** repo (vault rows), and explicitly exclude — with a stated reason — the pcap snapshot, whose protection is access-control + retention (ADR-0023 §5, ADR-0030 §3), not plaintext-absence. One sentence in ADR-0030 §3 or §5.4 closes this.

## F8 — Minor: missing bidirectional cross-references

**Where:** Whole documents. ADR-0030 cites ADR-0004/0005/0011/0023 but **not** ADR-0032, despite depending on the KMS KEK for its entire credential-separation argument and restore drill. ADR-0032 never mentions backup/restore/DR, despite F1/F2 being squarely its concern.

**Fix.** Add reciprocal references: ADR-0030 → ADR-0032 for the production KEK provider, rotation, and fail-closed behavior; ADR-0032 → ADR-0030 for backup-retention/KEK-version coupling (F1) and the combined DR drill (F2). Same-wave ADRs (both P1 W0) with a hard runtime dependency should not be mutually silent.

## D-decision alignment (confirmed consistent)

For the record, the following were checked and found **consistent** with the brief — no action needed:

- **D4 (Postgres = system of record):** ADR-0030 §1 correctly treats Postgres as the sole load-bearing restore tier; `config_snapshots`/`raw_artifacts` covered transitively (§4). ✔
- **D5 (Neo4j = rebuildable projection):** ADR-0030 §2 makes DR a re-projection, not a graph restore, and correctly treats the optional `neo4j-admin dump` as opt-in fast-start, matching D5 and PRODUCTION.md §8. ✔
- **D11 (AES-256-GCM envelope, master key env/file/KMS, append-only audit):** ADR-0032 extends — does not re-decide — the `KeyProvider` interface; schema (`wrapped_dek`, `kek_version`) unchanged; audit immutability (INSERT/SELECT grant + `BEFORE UPDATE OR DELETE` trigger) preserved and, in ADR-0030 §1/§5, asserted post-restore. ✔
- **D14 (pcap on disk volume, metadata + retention in Postgres):** ADR-0030 §3 correctly defers to ADR-0023 (the M5 realization of D14); the 30-day tombstone retention number matches ADR-0023 §4. ✔ (See F5/F7 for the *DR* edges of this, which are new surface, not D14 conflicts.)
- **D8 (Celery + Redis):** ADR-0030 §4's choice to run DR jobs on K8s CronJobs rather than Celery beat is a deliberate, well-justified departure for liveness independence — not a D8 conflict (D8 governs application jobs). ✔ (But see F5 for the residual coupling.)

## Recommended disposition

- **Block acceptance on F1 and F2** — both are silent DR-correctness defects that current drills would pass. They are joint obligations of ADR-0030 + ADR-0032 and should be resolved together (a single edit can close both: the full-platform DR drill restores the oldest retained backup, recovers the KEK from escrow, and decrypts a sample credential).
- **Resolve F3 before the P1 infra build** (`archive_timeout` is a one-line config decision that determines whether the PROPOSED RPO is testable at all).
- **F4–F8** are wording/scoping fixes that can land with the same revision; none requires a superseding ADR.
- All RPO/RTO/topology-RTO numbers remain correctly flagged **PROPOSED** pending the Consultant HA/DR answer (ADR-0030 §6, PRODUCTION.md §12) — no change needed there.
